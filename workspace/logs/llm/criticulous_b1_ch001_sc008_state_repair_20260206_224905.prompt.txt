The heavy oak doors of the Mayors office clicked shut behind me, the sound echoing through the marble-floored hallway like a judges gavel. [Quest Updated: The Mayors Cut]

I probably looked like a lunatica man in a linen tunic carrying a rusted piece of scrap metal and a glowing trophy that smelled vaguely of ozone and disappointment. I needed a plan. I needed gold. Most of all, I needed to not get breathed on too hard by anything with an offensive stat higher than zero.

I didn't even make it to the grand staircase before the air in the hallway changed. It grew heavy, smelling of expensive incense and polished steel.

He was tall, draped in a Silk Cloak that probably cost more than my entire life insurance policy back home, and he had the kind of jawline you could use to chip ice. Above his head, the System labels flickered in a golden hue that made my eyes ache.

[Vane - Lvl 28] [Class: Sun-Blessed Paladin] [Affiliation: Order of the Golden Ratio]

Twenty-eight. He was Level 28. I was Level 2. I was a 'Statistical Anomaly' with 1 HP. He was a literal god-king in comparison. 

Vane didn't move as I approached, but his eyes followed me. They weren't looking at my face; they were locked onto the flickering neon text that hovered above my head, the one the gate scanners had so graciously gifted me.

[Title: Statistical Anomaly]

We maintain the balance of the System. And usually, when something is as... unbalanced... as you appear to be, its my job to see if it needs a patch."

I felt a cold sweat break out on my neck. My 1 HP bar was invisible to himI hopedbut the [Statistical Anomaly] tag was a massive neon sign saying *'Examine Me.'*

"I don't care for gold," Vane said. "I care for data. The gate scanners reported a 100% Critical Hit rate. Statistically impossible. Even the most dedicated Luck-builds cap out at forty percent before the diminishing returns kick in. Youre a walking impossibility, Arthur."

"Artie," I corrected. "And its not impossible if the math allows it. Its just... unlikely."

"Is it?" Vane took a step forward. The hallway felt smaller. "I want to see it. A friendly spar. Just to see how the 'Anomaly' handles a real blade. Ill even set my damage output to 'Training' mode."

[Rules: First to 10% HP or First Yield.]

"I dont spar for free," I said, forcing a smirk I didn't feel. I shifted my weight, making sure the Participation Trophy was visible. "And honestly? Youre not geared for it."

"Youre a Paladin. High defense, high sustain, moderate burst," I said, leaning into the bluff Id used on the Mayor. "But youre looking at my HP and thinking Im a glass cannon. You think you can just tap me and the duel is over."

"Do you really think someone would walk into a Level 28 zone with 1 HP if they didn't have a way to make sure that 1 HP never moves? Have you ever heard of the 'Perfect Guard' build, Vane? Or are you too busy polishing your cape to read the high-level theory-crafting forums?"

Every hit you land on me while my guard is up doesn't just do zero damageit reflects 200% of the force back at the attacker. Thats why my HP is 1. Its a bait. A lure for people like you who think a small number means a weak player."
