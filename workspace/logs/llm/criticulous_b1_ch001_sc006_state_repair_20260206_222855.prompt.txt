Behind us, the Level 4 Tutorial Wolf let out a howl that sounded less like a predator and more like a dial-up modem screaming at a wall. It wasn't just the wolf. Thanks to the gold-plated monstrosity fused to my left hand, I had a trail of angry slimes, two disgruntled squirrels, and a very confused-looking badger following me like I was the messiah of the misunderstood.

The Participation Trophy hummed. It didn't just vibrate; it sang with the frequency of a thousand "You Tried" stickers.

"Math loves you, Artie! It just has a very aggressive way of showing it!" Fizz pointed ahead. "Look! The Oakhaven Safe Zone! If we cross the threshold, the aggro-leash should snap!"

The gates of Oakhaven weren't just wood and iron. They were a sprawling monument to the Systems obsession with order. Massive white stone pillars rose from the earth, inscribed with glowing blue runes that pulsed in time with the worlds heartbeat. Above the archway, a massive floating UI display read: [WELCOME TO OAKHAVEN - POPULATION: 1,242 (98.4% STABLE)].

I didn't care about the stability. I cared about the invisible line in the dirt.

I threw myself forward, my right hand white-knuckling the Broken Tutorial Sword. The wolf lunged, its jaws snapping inches from my Linen Tunic. If those teeth touched me, I was gone. One hit point. One. I was a soap bubble in a world made of needles.

I crossed the line.

*BZZZT.*

The wolf hit an invisible wall of shimmering blue force and bounced backward, its snarl turning into a series of glitched whimpers. The slimes and the badger followed suit, piling up against the barrier like a crowd at a sold-out concert.

[Entering: Oakhaven (Safe Zone)]
[Aggro Cleared. Welcome, Traveler.]

"We... we made it," I gasped, watching my stamina bar begin its agonizingly slow crawl back toward double digits.

"Don't get too comfortable," Fizz said, landing on my shoulder. "We still have to pass the Gate Scanner. And usually, the System likes its travelers to have, you know, more than one hit point and a luck stat that doesn't look like a typo."
