"Average? I'll have you know I once won a regional spelling bee," Artie said, his voice echoing in a way that suggested the 'room' he was in was roughly the size of forever.

[Spelling Bee: Irrelevant.]
[Data Entry Level 4: Pathetic.]
[Sarcasm Level 10: Noted.]

A massive, glowing screen manifested in the void before him. It was covered in sliders, numbers, and categories that made his head spin. At the top, in bold, flickering letters, it read:

[CHARACTER CREATION: PHASE 1]

"Character creation?" Artie squinted at the screen. "Is this heaven? Because if heaven is a UI, I’m going to be very disappointed."

[Heaven is currently undergoing maintenance. You have been redirected to the System Integration Hub.]
[Please select your starting attributes. Note: Due to the 'Vending Machine Incident,' your account has been flagged for 'Anomalous Death Bonus.']

Artie drifted closer to the screen. He saw Strength, Agility, Intelligence—the usual suspects. They were all set to 10. Balanced. Boring. 

"Anomalous Death Bonus, huh?" Artie reached out a translucent finger. "Does that mean I get better chips?"

[Bonus applies to 'Luck' and 'Critical' sub-routines.]
[Warning: Manual adjustment of stats is permanent. Please choose wisely.]

Artie looked at his 10 HP. It looked so small. So fragile. He looked at the 'Crit' stat, which was currently a tiny, flickering icon at the bottom of the screen.

"If I’m going to be stuck in a game," Artie muttered, "I might as well make it interesting. Nobody ever remembers the guy who played it safe."

He reached for the sliders, but before he could touch them, the void began to shake again. A voice, much louder and far less polite than the System notifications, boomed through the emptiness.

"USER 992-DELTA! YOU ARE LATE FOR YOUR ALLOCATION!"

Artie spun around—or tried to—but there was nothing to see but the white light of the screen. 

"I'm working on it!" Artie shouted back. "The UI is clunky!"

[Alert: External Interference Detected.]
[Accelerating Character Creation...]

The sliders began to move on their own, dancing to the rhythm of the void's tremors. Artie watched in horror as his Strength dropped to 1. His Agility dropped to 1. His Intelligence, his Stamina, his Charisma—all of them plummeted toward the bottom of the scale.

"Hey! Stop that! I didn't click that!"

[Points Reallocated to: CRITICAL HIT RATE]
[Current Rate: 100%]

"One hundred percent?" Artie stared at the screen. "What about my health? What about my defense?"

[HP: 1/1]

The screen flashed a blinding, violent red. 

[Build Finalized: THE GLASS CANNON]
[Title Acquired: Statistical Anomaly]

"One HP? You're sending me into a world with one HP?" Artie screamed at the void. "A stiff breeze will kill me! A slightly aggressive squirrel will be my boss fight!"

[Integration in 3... 2... 1...]

The void collapsed. The silence was replaced by the chirping of birds, the rustle of leaves, and the terrifyingly loud sound of Artie’s own heartbeat. 

He wasn't in the breakroom anymore. And he definitely wasn't in accounting.
